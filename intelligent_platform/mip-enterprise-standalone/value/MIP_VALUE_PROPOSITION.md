# MIP — Value Proposition
### Why the Mainframe Intelligence Platform is the *backbone* of modernization, not another AI tool

*A leave-behind for the conversation after the meeting.*

---

## The category, in one sentence
> **MIP is not a tool that generates code. It is the system-of-record and verification backbone an
> entire modernization program runs on — the layer that knows what the estate *is*, and proves that
> anything built from it is *correct*.**

AI tools **generate**. MIP **knows, and verifies.** That is a different category — closer to a
*control plane* or a *digital twin* of the mainframe than to a code assistant.

## The problem it actually solves
Every mainframe modernization starts in the dark. Decades of COBOL/JCL/DB2/CICS, the authors retired,
the docs wrong or gone, dependencies hidden behind dynamic calls and copybooks. So teams **scope
blind, sequence blind, and rewrite blind** — which is why these programs overrun, stall, or break
production. **The bottleneck was never writing new code. It is understanding the old code, and
proving the new code behaves identically.**

## The strategic wedge (why this *wins*)
> **As AI makes code generation cheap, understanding and verification become the bottleneck — and the
> value.** When anyone can generate Java, the only questions that matter are *"is it correct?"* and
> *"did we cover everything?"* That is exactly what MIP answers.

So MIP doesn't compete with the AI-codegen wave — **it is what makes that wave safe to ride.**
Without MIP, AI modernization is *confident hallucination at scale.* MIP's value therefore **rises**
as codegen commoditizes.

## What it does — across the whole program (breadth = coverage, not feature creep)

| Phase | Capability | The outcome |
|---|---|---|
| **See** | Inventory, classification, call/lineage/flow graphs | Make the invisible estate visible |
| **Understand** | Roots, clusters, **customer journeys**, capabilities/domains | Scope & sequence from facts, not guesses |
| **Capture** | Business rules + evidence/confidence on every fact | Preserve SME knowledge *before they retire* |
| **Decide** | Service candidates, risk-ordered roadmap | What to carve out, in what order, how risky |
| **Rebuild** | Confirmed requirements → grounded Java | AI generation that's anchored, not hallucinated |
| **Prove** | Dual-run / golden-master equivalence, scorecards | Cut over only when behavior matches |

MIP is the connective tissue across all six — the others are stages *on its rails.*

## The moat — four things an AI tool structurally cannot claim
1. **The evidence envelope** — every fact is cited, confidence-scored, and honest about uncertainty.
   *The only modernization platform that can prove its output is correct.*
2. **The knowledge graph is a durable, compounding asset** — it outlives any model, tool, or vendor;
   every workstream draws from it and enriches it.
3. **Verifiable equivalence** — it doesn't just write Java, it *proves* the Java behaves like the
   mainframe (to the penny).
4. **It is the integration point** — LLMs, codegen engines, SIs are interchangeable workers; **MIP is
   the rails.**

## Value by stakeholder
- **CIO / Board** — de-risk a multi-year program; **capture irreplaceable SME knowledge before it
  walks out the door**; spend with evidence, not faith.
- **Enterprise Architect** — a vendor-neutral control plane; *any* codegen tool or SI plugs in and is
  verified by it; decompose to services safely.
- **Delivery lead / SI** — discovery in weeks not months; a safe strangler sequence instead of boiling
  the ocean.
- **Risk / Compliance** — every decision traces to a line of code; every rewrite is proven
  behavior-equivalent.

## Why "another AI tool" is the wrong label
| "Another AI tool" | MIP |
|---|---|
| Generates plausible output | Records *proven* facts with evidence |
| Can't tell you what it doesn't know | Confidence + `needs_review` on everything uncertain |
| One-shot, disposable | A persistent knowledge graph the whole program runs on |
| "Trust me" | "Here's the cited source line, and the dual-run proof" |
| Replaced by the next model | The rails the next model plugs into |

## Proof points to demand (and that MIP can show)
Extraction **precision/recall** vs. ground truth · **coverage %** of the estate · % of customer
journeys **SME-confirmed** · **equivalence pass-rate** (Java == mainframe) · every fact traceable to a
file and line. *Metrics say platform; screenshots say tool.*

## The lines that land

- *"We don't replace your modernization program. We're the backbone it runs on."*

## The bottom line
Modernization fails because nobody can see the system, capture its knowledge before it's lost, or
prove the replacement is correct. **MIP is the backbone that does all three** — and it is the reason
AI can finally be used on the mainframe without betting the business on a guess.
